十九世纪欧洲的人口增长情况

Population Growth in Nineteenth-Century Europe
十九世纪欧洲的人口增长情况

Because of industrialization, but also because of a vast increase in agricultural output without which industrialization would have been impossible, Western Europeans by the latter half of the nineteenth century enjoyed higher standards of living and longer, healthier lives than most of the world’s peoples
由于工业化，同时也由于农业产出的大量增加，没有农业产出就不可能实现工业化，到19世纪后半期，西欧人比世界上大多数人享有更高的生活水平和更长、更健康的生命。
In Europe as a whole, the population rose from 188 million in 1800 to 400 million in 1900
在整个欧洲，人口从1800年的1.88亿上升到1900年的4亿。
By 1900, virtually every area of Europe had contributed to the tremendous surge of population, but each major region was at a different stage of demographic change
到1900年，欧洲几乎每个地区都为人口的巨大激增做出了贡献，但每个主要地区都处于人口变化的不同阶段。

Improvements in the food supply continued trends that had started in the late seventeenth century
食品供应的改善延续了17世纪末开始的趋势
New lands were put under cultivation, while the use of crops of American origin, particularly the potato, continued to expand
新的土地被投入耕种，而源自美国的作物，特别是马铃薯的使用继续扩大。
Setbacks did occur
确实发生了挫折 确实出现了挫折 挫折确实发生了 确实有挫折发生
Regional agricultural failures were the most common cause of economic recessions until 1850, and they could lead to localized famine as well
在1850年之前，地区性的农业失败是经济衰退最常见的原因，它们也可能导致局部的饥荒。
A major potato blight (disease) in 1846-1847 led to the deaths of at least one million persons in Ireland and the emigration of another million, and Ireland never recovered the population levels the potato had sustained to that point
1846-1847年的一次重大的马铃薯枯萎病（疾病）导致爱尔兰至少有100万人死亡，另有100万人移居国外，爱尔兰再也没有恢复到马铃薯到那时所维持的人口水平
Bad grain harvests at the same time led to increased hardship throughout much of Europe
同时，粮食歉收导致整个欧洲大部分地区的困苦加剧

After 1850, however, the expansion of foods more regularly kept pace with population growth, though the poorer classes remained malnourished
然而，在1850年之后，食品的扩张更经常地与人口增长保持同步，尽管较贫穷的阶层仍然营养不良。
Two developments were crucial
有两个发展是至关重要的 有两项发展是至关重要的 有两个发展是关键的 有两个事态发展是至关重要的
First, the application of science and new technology to agriculture increased
首先，科学和新技术在农业中的应用增加了
Led by German universities, increasing research was devoted to improving seeds, developing chemical fertilizers, and advancing livestock
在德国大学的带领下，越来越多的研究致力于改进种子、开发化肥和推进畜牧业的发展。
After 1861, with the development of land-grant universities in the United States that had huge agricultural programs, American crop-production research added to this mix
1861年后，随着美国拥有庞大农业项目的赠地大学的发展，美国的作物生产研究也加入了这一组合。
Mechanization included the use of horse-drawn harvesters and seed drills, many developed initially in the United States
机械化包括使用马拉的收割机和播种机，其中许多最初是在美国开发的。
It also included mechanical cream separators and other food-processing devices that improved supply
它还包括机械奶油分离器和其他改善供应的食品加工装置。

The second development involved industrially based transportation
第二个发展涉及基于工业的运输
With trains and steam shipping, it became possible to move foods to needy regions within Western Europe quickly
有了火车和蒸汽船，就有可能将食物迅速运送到西欧的贫困地区。
Famine (as opposed to malnutrition) became a thing of the past
饥荒（相对于营养不良）成为过去的事情
Many Western European countries, headed by Britain, began also to import increasing amounts of food, not only from Eastern Europe, a traditional source, but also from the Americas, Australia, and New Zealand
以英国为首的许多西欧国家也开始进口越来越多的食品，不仅从东欧这个传统来源，而且还从美洲、澳大利亚和新西兰进口。
Steam shipping, which improved speed and capacity, as well as new procedures for canning and refrigerating foods (particularly after 1870), was fundamental to these developments
蒸汽运输提高了速度和容量，以及罐装和冷藏食品的新程序（特别是在1870年之后），是这些发展的基础。

Europe’s population growth included one additional innovation by the nineteenth century: it combined with rapid urbanization
到19世纪，欧洲的人口增长还包括一项创新：它与快速的城市化相结合
More and more Western Europeans moved from countryside to city, and big cities grew most rapidly of all
越来越多的西欧人从乡村迁往城市，而大城市的发展最为迅速
By 1850, over half of all the people in England lived in cities, a first in human history
到1850年，英国一半以上的人生活在城市，这是人类历史上的第一次。
In one sense, this pattern seems inevitable growing numbers of people pressed available resources on the land, even when farmwork was combined with a bit of manufacturing, so people crowded into cities seeking work or other resources
从某种意义上说，这种模式似乎是不可避免的，越来越多的人压榨着土地上的可用资源，即使是在农业劳动与一些制造业相结合的情况下，人们也会挤进城市寻找工作或其他资源。
Traditionally, however, death rates in cities surpassed those in the countryside by a large margin; cities had maintained population only through steady in-migration
然而，传统上，城市的死亡率远远超过了农村的死亡率；城市只能通过稳定的移民来维持人口。
Thus rapid urbanization should have reduced overall population growth, but by the middle of the nineteenth century this was no longer the case
因此，快速的城市化应该减少总的人口增长，但到了19世纪中期，情况就不再是这样了
Urban death rates remained high, particularly in the lower-class slums, but they began to decline rapidly
城市的死亡率仍然很高，特别是在下层贫民窟，但它们开始迅速下降

The greater reliability of food supplies was a factor in the decline of urban death rates
食物供应更加可靠是城市死亡率下降的一个因素
Even more important were the gains in urban sanitation, as well as measures such as inspection of housing
更重要的是在城市卫生方面取得的成果，以及对住房进行检查等措施。
Reformers, including enlightened doctors, began to study the causes of high death rates and to urge remediation
改革者，包括开明的医生，开始研究高死亡率的原因，并敦促进行补救。
Even before the discovery of germs, beliefs that disease spread by “miasmas” (noxious forms of bad air) prompted attention to sewers and open garbage; Edwin Chadwick led an exemplary urban crusade for underground sewers in England in the 1830s
甚至在发现病菌之前，认为疾病通过 “瘴气”（有毒的不良空气形式）传播的信念就促使人们关注下水道和露天垃圾；埃德温-查德威克在19世纪30年代领导了一场关于英国地下下水道的模范城市运动。
Gradually, public health provisions began to cut into customary urban mortality rates
渐渐地，公共卫生规定开始削减城市的习惯死亡率
By 1900, in some parts of Western Europe life expectancy in the cities began to surpass that of the rural areas
到1900年，在西欧的一些地区，城市的预期寿命开始超过农村地区的预期寿命。
Industrial societies had figured out ways to combine large and growing cities with population growth, a development that would soon spread to other parts of the world
工业社会已经想出了将大型和不断增长的城市与人口增长相结合的方法，这一发展很快就会蔓延到世界其他地区。
