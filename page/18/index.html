金星和地球上的表面液体

Surface Fluids on Venus and Earth
金星和地球上的表面液体

A fluid is a substance, such as a liquid or gas, in which the component particles (usually molecules) can move past one another
流体是一种物质，如液体或气体，其中的组成颗粒（通常是分子）可以相互移动。
Fluids flow easily and conform to the shape of their containers
液体容易流动并符合其容器的形状
The geologic processes related to the movement of fluids on a planet’s surface can completely resurface a planet many times
与行星表面流体运动有关的地质过程可以使一个行星完全重新浮出水面许多次
These processes derive their energy from the Sun and the gravitational forces of the planet itself
这些过程从太阳和行星本身的引力中获得能量
As these fluids interact with surface materials, they move particles about or react chemically with them to modify or produce materials
当这些液体与表面材料相互作用时，它们会使颗粒移动或与它们发生化学反应，从而改变或产生材料。
On a solid planet with a hydrosphere and an atmosphere, only a tiny fraction of the planetary mass flows as surface fluids
在一个有水圈和大气的固体行星上，只有极小部分的行星质量以表面流体的形式流动。
Yet the movements of these fluids can drastically alter a planet
然而，这些流体的运动可以极大地改变一个星球。
Consider Venus and Earth, both terrestrial planets with atmospheres
考虑到金星和地球，这两颗陆地行星都有大气层

Venus and Earth are commonly regarded as twin planets but not identical twins
金星和地球通常被认为是双胞胎行星，但不是同卵双胞胎。
They are about the same size, are composed of roughly the same mix of materials, and may have been comparably endowed at their beginning with carbon dioxide and water
它们的大小差不多，由大致相同的材料组合组成，并且在开始时可能具有相当的二氧化碳和水。
However, the twins evolved differently largely because of differences in their distance from the Sun
然而，这对双胞胎的进化方式不同，主要是因为它们与太阳的距离不同。
With a significant amount of internal heat, Venus may continue to be geologically active with volcanoes, rifting, and folding
由于有大量的内部热量，金星可能会继续在地质上活跃，出现火山、断裂和褶皱等现象。
However, it lacks any sign of a hydrologic system (water circulation and distribution): there are no streams, lakes oceans or glaciers
然而，它缺乏任何水文系统（水循环和分配）的迹象：没有溪流、湖泊、海洋或冰川。
Space probes suggest that Venus may have started with as much water as Earth, but it was unable to keep its water in liquid form
空间探测器表明，金星开始时可能有和地球一样多的水，但它无法将其水保持在液态形式。
Because Venus receives more heat from the Sun, water released from the interior evaporated and rose to the upper atmosphere where the Sun’s ultraviolet rays broke the molecules apart
由于金星从太阳接收到更多的热量，从内部释放的水蒸发并上升到大气层上部，在那里太阳的紫外线将分子分解开来
Much of the freed hydrogen escaped into space, and Venus lost its water
大部分被释放的氢气逃到了太空中，而金星失去了它的水。
Without water, Venus became less and less like Earth and kept an atmosphere filled with carbon dioxide
没有水，金星变得越来越不像地球，并保持着充满二氧化碳的大气层
The carbon dioxide acts as a blanket, creating an intense greenhouse effect and driving surface temperatures high enough to melt lead and to prohibit the formation of carbonate minerals
二氧化碳就像一张毯子，产生强烈的温室效应，使表面温度高到足以融化铅，并禁止碳酸盐矿物的形成。
Volcanoes continually vented more carbon dioxide into the atmosphere
火山不断地将更多的二氧化碳排放到大气中
On Earth, liquid water removes carbon dioxide from the atmosphere and combines it with calcium, from rock weathering, to form carbonate sedimentary rocks
在地球上，液态水从大气中带走二氧化碳，并与岩石风化产生的钙结合，形成碳酸盐沉积岩。
Without liquid water to remove carbon from the atmosphere, the level of carbon dioxide in the atmosphere of Venus remains high
如果没有液态水来清除大气中的碳，金星大气中的二氧化碳水平仍然很高

Like Venus, Earth is large enough to be geologically active and for its gravitational field to hold an atmosphere
像金星一样，地球大到足以让地质活动活跃，其引力场足以容纳大气。
Unlike Venus, it is just the right distance from the Sun so that temperature ranges allow water to exist as a liquid, a solid, and a gas
与金星不同，它与太阳的距离恰到好处，因此温度范围允许水以液体、固体和气体的形式存在。
Water is thus extremely mobile and moves rapidly over the planet in a continuous hydrologic cycle
因此，水具有极强的流动性，在连续的水文循环中在地球上迅速移动
Heated by the Sun, the water moves in great cycles from the oceans to the atmosphere, over the landscape in river systems, and ultimately back to the oceans
在太阳的加热下，水在巨大的循环中从海洋流向大气层，在河流系统中穿过景观，并最终回到海洋中。
As a result, Earth’s surface has been continually changed and eroded into delicate systems of river valleys - a remarkable contrast to the surfaces of other planetary bodies where impact craters dominate
因此，地球的表面被不断地改变，并被侵蚀成精致的河谷系统–这与其他行星体的表面形成了显著的对比，这些行星体的撞击坑占主导地位。
Few areas on Earth have been untouched by flowing water
地球上很少有地区没有被流动的水触动过 地球上很少有地区没有被流动的水影响过 地球上很少有地区没有被流水冲刷过 地球上很少有地区没有被流动的水所影响
As a result, river valleys are the dominant feature of its landscape
因此，河谷是其景观的主要特征。
Similarly, wind action has scoured fine particles away from large areas, depositing them elsewhere as vast sand seas dominated by dunes or in sheets of loess (fine-grained soil deposits)
同样，风的作用将细小的颗粒从大片地区吹走，在其他地方沉积成以沙丘为主的巨大沙海或成片的黄土（细粒土壤沉积）。
These fluid movements are caused by gravity flow systems energized by heat from the Sun
这些流体运动是由太阳的热量激发的重力流系统引起的
Other geologic changes occur when the gases in the atmosphere or water react with rocks at the surface to form new chemical compounds with different properties
当大气中的气体或水与地表的岩石发生反应，形成具有不同性质的新化合物时，就会发生其他地质变化。
An important example of this process was the removal of most of Earths carbon dioxide from its atmosphere to form carbonate rocks
这一过程的一个重要例子是将地球上的大部分二氧化碳从大气中移除，形成碳酸盐岩石。
However, if Earth were a little closer to the Sun, its oceans would evaporate; if it were farther from the Sun, the oceans would freeze solid
然而，如果地球离太阳再近一点，它的海洋就会蒸发；如果它离太阳再远一点，海洋就会冻结成固体。
Because liquid water was present, self-replicating molecules of carbon, hydrogen, and oxygen developed life early in Earth’s history and have radically modified its surface, blanketing huge parts of the continents with greenery
由于液态水的存在，碳、氢和氧的自我复制分子在地球历史的早期发展了生命，并从根本上改变了地球的表面，使大陆的大部分地区被绿色植物所覆盖。
Life thrives on this planet, and it helped create the planet’s oxygen- and nitrogen-rich atmosphere and moderate temperatures
生命在这个星球上茁壮成长，它帮助创造了这个星球上富含氧气和氮气的大气和适度的温度

纳图菲亚人的文化

Natufian Culture
纳图菲亚人的文化

In the archaeological record of the Natufian period, from about 12,500 to 10,200 years ago, in the part of the Middle East known as the Levant - roughly east of the Mediterranean and north of the Arabian Peninsula - we see clear evidence of agricultural origins
在纳图菲亚时期的考古记录中，从大约12500年到10200年前，在被称为黎凡特的中东地区–大致在地中海以东和阿拉伯半岛以北–我们看到农业起源的明确证据
The stone tools of the Natufians included many sickle-shaped cutting blades that show a pattern of wear characteristic of cereal harvesting
纳图菲人的石器包括许多镰刀状的切割刀片，显示出谷物收割时特有的磨损模式。
Also, querns (hand mills) and other stone tools used for processing grain occur in abundance at Natufian sites, and many such tools show signs of long, intensive use
此外，在纳图菲亚遗址中还出现了大量用于加工谷物的querns（手磨）和其他石制工具，而且许多此类工具显示出长期密集使用的迹象。
Along with the sickle blades are many grinding stones, primarily mortars and pestles of limestone or basalt
与镰刀刃一起的还有许多磨石，主要是石灰石或玄武岩的臼和杵。
There is also evidence that these heavy grinding stones were transported over long distances, more than 30 kilometers in some cases, and this is not something known to have been done by people of preceding periods
还有证据表明，这些沉重的磨石被长途运输，在某些情况下超过30公里，而这并不是已知的前几个时期的人们所做的事情。
Fishhooks and weights for sinking fishing nets attest to the growing importance of fish in the diet in some areas
鱼钩和下沉渔网的砝码证明了鱼类在一些地区的饮食中越来越重要。
Stone vessels indicate an increased need for containers, but there is no evidence of Natufian clay working or pottery
石器表明对容器的需求增加，但没有证据表明纳图菲亚时代的粘土加工或制陶。
Studies of the teeth of Natufians also strongly suggest that these people specialized in collecting cereals and may have been cultivating them and in the process of domesticating them, but they were also still hunter-foragers who intensively hunted gazelle and deer in more lush areas and wild goats and equids in more arid zones
对纳图菲亚人牙齿的研究也强烈表明，这些人专门收集谷物，并可能一直在栽培谷物，而且正在驯化谷物，但他们也仍然是狩猎者，在较为茂盛的地区密集地猎取羚羊和鹿，在较为干旱的地区猎取野山羊和马。

The Natufians had a different settlement pattern from that of their predecessors
纳图菲人的定居模式与他们的前辈不同
Some of their base camps were far larger (over 1,000 square meters) than any of those belonging to earlier periods, and they may have lived in some of these camps for half the year or even more
他们的一些大本营比早期的任何一个大本营都要大得多（超过1000平方米），而且他们可能在其中一些大本营中生活了半年甚至更长时间。
In some of the camps, people made foundations and other architectural elements out of limestone blocks
在一些营地，人们用石灰石块制作地基和其他建筑元素。
Trade in shell, obsidian, and other commodities seems to have been on the rise, and anthropologists suspect that the exchange of perishables (such as skins, foodstuffs) and salt was also on the increase
贝壳、黑曜石和其他商品的贸易似乎一直在增加，人类学家怀疑易腐物品（如皮毛、食品）和盐的交换也在增加。
With the growing importance of wild cereals in the diet, salt probably became for the first time a near necessity: people who eat a lot of meat get many essential salts from this diet, but diets based on cereals can be deficient in salts
随着野生谷物在饮食中的重要性日益增加，盐可能第一次成为一种近乎必需品：吃很多肉的人从这种饮食中获得许多必需的盐，但以谷物为主的饮食可能缺乏盐。
Salt was probably also important as a food preservative in early villages
在早期的村庄，盐可能也是重要的食品防腐剂。

As always, there is more to a major cultural change than simply a shift in economics
与往常一样，重大的文化变革不仅仅是经济上的转变，还有更多的内容。
The Natufians made (and presumably wore) beads and pendants in many materials, including gemstones and marine shells that had to be imported, and it is possible that this ornamentation actually reflects a growing sense of ethnic identity and perhaps some differences in personal and group status
纳图菲人用许多材料制作（并可能佩戴）珠子和吊坠，包括必须进口的宝石和海洋贝壳，可能这种装饰品实际上反映了日益增长的种族认同感，也许还有一些个人和团体地位的差异
Cleverly carved figurines of animals, women, and other subjects occur in many sites, and Natufian period cave paintings have been found in Anatolia, Syria, and Iran
在许多遗址中出现了巧妙的动物、妇女和其他主题的雕像，在安纳托利亚、叙利亚和伊朗也发现了纳图菲亚时期的洞穴壁画。
More than 400 Natufian burials have been found, most of them simple graves set in house floors
已经发现了400多座纳图菲人的墓葬，其中大部分是设置在房屋地板上的简单坟墓。
As archaeologist Belfer-Cohen notes, these burials may reflect an ancestor cult and a growing sense of community emotional ties and attachment to a particular place, and toward the end of the Natufian period, people in this area were making a strict separation between living quarters and burial grounds
正如考古学家Belfer-Cohen所指出的，这些墓葬可能反映了对祖先的崇拜和日益增长的社区情感纽带和对特定地方的依恋，在Natufian时期的末期，这个地区的人们正在将生活区和墓地严格分开。
In contrast with the Pleistocene cultures of the Levant, Natufian culture appears to have experienced considerable social change
与黎凡特的更新世文化相比，纳图菲亚文化似乎经历了相当大的社会变化

The question of why the Natufians differed from their predecessors in these and other ways and why they made these first steps toward farming as a way of life remains unclear
为什么纳图菲人在这些方面和其他方面与他们的前辈不同，以及为什么他们向农耕这一生活方式迈出了第一步，这个问题仍然不清楚。
There were climate changes, of course, and growing aridity and rising population densities may have forced them to intensify the exploitation of cereals, which in turn might have stimulated the development of sickles and other tools and the permanent communities that make agriculture efficient
当然，还有气候变化，日益干旱和人口密度的增加可能迫使他们加强对谷物的开发，这反过来可能刺激了镰刀和其他工具的发展，以及使农业高效的永久社区的发展。
But precisely how these factors interacted with others at play is poorly understood
但确切地说，这些因素如何与其他因素相互作用，人们对此了解甚少。

木材的商业化

The Commercialization of Lumber
木材的商业化
In nineteenth-century America, practically everything that was built involved wood
在十九世纪的美国，几乎所有的建筑都涉及到木材。
Pine was especially attractive for building purposes
松树在建筑方面特别有吸引力 松树在建筑方面特别具有吸引力 松树在建筑方面尤其具有吸引力 松树在建筑方面尤其有吸引力
It is durable and strong, yet soft enough to be easily worked with even the simplest of hand tools
它耐用而坚固，但又足够柔软，甚至可以用最简单的手工工具轻松加工。
It also floats nicely on water, which allowed it to be transported to distant markets across the nation
它还能很好地漂浮在水面上，这使得它能够被运往全国各地的遥远市场。
The central and northern reaches of the Great Lakes states—Michigan, Wisconsin, and Minnesota—all contained extensive pine forests as well as many large rivers for floating logs into the Great Lakes, from where they were transported nationwide
大湖区各州的中部和北部–密歇根州、威斯康星州和明尼苏达州–都有广泛的松树林以及许多大河，可以将原木漂流到大湖区，从那里运往全国各地。

By 1860, the settlement of the American West along with timber shortages in the East converged with ever-widening impact on the pine forests of the Great Lakes states
到1860年，美国西部的定居和东部的木材短缺对大湖区各州的松树林产生了越来越广泛的影响。
Over the next 30 years, lumbering became a full-fledged enterprise in Michigan, Wisconsin, and Minnesota
在接下来的30年里，伐木业在密歇根州、威斯康星州和明尼苏达州成为一个全面的企业。
Newly formed lumbering corporations bought up huge tracts of pineland and set about systematically cutting the trees
新成立的伐木公司买下了大片的松树地，开始有计划地砍伐树木。
Both the colonists and the later industrialists saw timber as a commodity, but the latter group adopted a far more thorough and calculating approach to removing trees
殖民者和后来的工业家都把木材看作是一种商品，但后者采取了一种更彻底和更有计划的方法来移除树木。
In this sense, what happened between 1860 and 1890 represented a significant break with the past
在这个意义上，1860年至1890年间发生的事情代表了与过去的重大决裂
No longer were farmers in search of extra income the main source for shingles, firewood, and other wood products
寻求额外收入的农民不再是瓦片、木柴和其他木制品的主要来源。
By the 1870s, farmers and city dwellers alike purchased forest products from large manufacturing companies located in the Great Lakes states rather than chopping wood themselves or buying it locally
到19世纪70年代，农民和城市居民都从位于大湖区各州的大型制造公司购买森林产品，而不是自己砍伐木材或在当地购买。

The commercialization of lumbering was in part the product of technological change
伐木业的商业化在一定程度上是技术变革的产物。
The early, thick saw blades tended to waste a large quantity of wood, with perhaps as much as a third of the log left behind on the floor as sawdust or scrap
早期厚重的锯片往往会浪费大量的木材，可能有多达三分之一的原木作为锯末或废料留在地板上。
In the 1870s, however, the British-invented band saw, with its thinner blade, became standard issue in the Great Lakes states’ lumber factories
然而，在19世纪70年代，英国发明的带锯及其较薄的锯片，成为大湖区各州木材厂的标准配置。
Meanwhile, the rise of steam-powered mills streamlined production by allowing for the more efficient, centralized, and continuous cutting of lumber
同时，蒸汽动力工厂的兴起使生产更加合理，使木材的切割更加高效、集中和连续。
Steam helped to automate a variety of tasks, from cutting to the carrying away of waste
蒸汽帮助实现了各种任务的自动化，从切割到运走废物。
Mills also employed steam to heat log ponds, preventing them from freezing and making possible year-round lumber production
磨坊还利用蒸汽来加热原木池，防止其结冰，使全年的木材生产成为可能。

For industrial lumbering to succeed, a way had to be found to neutralize the effects of the seasons on production
为了使工业化伐木获得成功，必须找到一种方法来中和季节对生产的影响。
Traditionally, cutting took place in the winter, when snow and ice made it easier to drag logs on sleds or sleighs to the banks of streams
传统上，砍伐是在冬季进行的，当时冰雪使人们更容易用雪橇或雪橇将原木拖到溪流的岸边。
Once the streams and lakes thawed, workers rafted the logs to mills, where they were cut into lumber in the summer
一旦溪流和湖泊解冻，工人们就用筏子把原木运到工厂，在夏天将它们切割成木材。
If nature did not cooperate—if the winter proved dry and warm, if the spring thaw was delayed—production would suffer
如果大自然不配合–如果冬天被证明是干燥和温暖的，如果春天的解冻被推迟–生产将受到影响。
To counter the effects of climate on lumber production, loggers experimented with a variety of techniques for transporting trees out of the woods
为了应对气候对木材生产的影响，伐木者尝试了各种将树木运出森林的技术
