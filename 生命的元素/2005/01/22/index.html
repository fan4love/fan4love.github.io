生命的元素

Elements of Life
生命的元素

The creation of life requires a set of chemical elements for making the components of cells
生命的创造需要一套化学元素来制造细胞的组成部分
Life on Earth uses about 25 of the 92 naturally occurring chemical elements, although just 4 of these elements—oxygen, carbon, hydrogen, and nitrogen—make up about 96 percent of the mass of living organisms
地球上的生命使用92种自然发生的化学元素中的大约25种，尽管这些元素中只有4种–氧、碳、氢和氮–构成了生物体质量的大约96%。
Thus, a first requirement for life might be the presence of most or all of the elements used by life
因此，生命的第一个要求可能是存在生命所使用的大部分或全部元素

Interestingly, this requirement can probably be met by almost any world
有趣的是，这个要求可能几乎可以被任何世界上的人所满足
Scientists have determined that all chemical elements in the universe besides hydrogen and helium (and a trace amount of lithium) were produced by stars
科学家们已经确定，宇宙中除了氢和氦（以及微量的锂）之外的所有化学元素都是由恒星产生的。
These are known as heavy elements because they are heavier than hydrogen and helium
这些元素被称为重元素，因为它们比氢和氦更重。
Although all of these heavy elements are quite rare compared to hydrogen and helium, they are found just about everywhere
尽管与氢和氦相比，所有这些重元素都相当罕见，但它们几乎随处可见。

Heavy elements are continually being manufactured by stars and released into space by stellar deaths, so their amount compared to hydrogen and helium gradually rises with time
重元素不断被恒星制造出来，并因恒星死亡而被释放到太空中，因此它们的数量与氢和氦相比，会随着时间的推移而逐渐上升。
Heavy elements make up about 2 percent of the chemical content (by mass) of our solar system, the other 98 percent is hydrogen and helium
重元素约占我们太阳系化学成分（按质量）的2%，其他98%是氢和氦。
In some very old star systems, which formed before many heavy elements were produced, the heavy-element share may be less than 0
在一些非常古老的恒星系统中，它们在许多重元素产生之前就已经形成了，重元素的比例可能低于0
1 percent
百分之1 1个百分点 1％的人 1％的
Nevertheless, every star system studied has at least some amount of all the elements used by life
然而，所研究的每一个恒星系统至少都有一定数量的生命所使用的所有元素。
Moreover, when planetesimals—small, solid objects formed in the early solar system that may accumulate to become planets—condense within a forming star system, they are inevitably made from heavy elements because the more common hydrogen and helium remain gaseous
此外，当小行星–早期太阳系中形成的、可能积累成行星的小型固体物体–在形成中的恒星系统中凝结时，它们不可避免地由重元素构成，因为更常见的氢和氦仍然是气态的。
Thus, planetesimals everywhere should contain the elements needed for life, which means that objects built from planetesimals—planets, moons, asteroids, and comets-also contain these elements
因此，各地的小行星应该含有生命所需的元素，这意味着由小行星建造的物体–行星、卫星、小行星和彗星–也含有这些元素。
The nature of solar-system formation explains why Earth contains all the elements needed for life, and it is why we expect these elements to be present on other worlds throughout our solar system, galaxy, and universe
太阳系形成的性质解释了为什么地球含有生命所需的所有元素，这也是为什么我们预计这些元素会出现在整个太阳系、银河系和宇宙的其他世界上的原因。

Note that this argument does not change, even if we allow for life very different from life on Earth
请注意，即使我们允许有与地球上的生命非常不同的生命，这个论点也不会改变。
Life on Earth is carbon based, and most biologists believe that life elsewhere is likely to be carbon based as well
地球上的生命是以碳为基础的，大多数生物学家认为，其他地方的生命也可能是以碳为基础的。
However, we cannot absolutely rule out the possibility of life with another chemical basis, such as silicon or nitrogen
然而，我们不能绝对排除有另一种化学基础的生命的可能性，如硅或氮。
The set of elements (or their relative proportions) used by life based on some other element might be somewhat different from that used by carbon-based life on Earth
基于其他元素的生命所使用的元素集（或其相对比例）可能与地球上基于碳的生命所使用的元素集有些不同
But the elements are still products of stars and would still be present in planetesimals everywhere
但这些元素仍然是恒星的产物，仍然会存在于各地的行星上。
No matter what kinds of life we are looking for, we are likely to find the necessary elements on almost every planet, moon, asteroid, and comet in the universe
无论我们在寻找什么样的生命，我们都有可能在宇宙中几乎每一颗行星、月球、小行星和彗星上找到必要的元素。

A somewhat stricter requirement is the presence of these elements in molecules that can be used as ready-made building blocks for life, just as early Earth probably had an organic soup of amino acids and other complex molecules
一个更严格的要求是这些元素在分子中的存在，可以作为生命的现成构件，就像早期地球可能有氨基酸和其他复杂分子的有机汤。
Earth’s organic molecules likely came from some combination of three sources: chemical reactions in the atmosphere, chemical reactions near deep-sea vents in the oceans, and molecules carried to Earth by asteroids and comets
地球上的有机分子可能来自三个来源的某种组合：大气中的化学反应，海洋中深海喷口附近的化学反应，以及由小行星和彗星带到地球的分子。
The first two sources can occur only on worlds with atmospheres or oceans, respectively
前两个来源只可能发生在有大气层或海洋的世界上，分别为
But the third source should have brought similar molecules to nearly all worlds in our solar system
但第三个来源应该给我们太阳系的几乎所有世界带来了类似的分子

Studies of meteorites and comets suggest that organic molecules are widespread among both asteroids and comets
对陨石和彗星的研究表明，有机分子在小行星和彗星中都很普遍
Because each body in the solar system was repeatedly struck by asteroids and comets during the period known as the heavy bombardment (about 4 billion years ago), each body should have received at least some organic molecules
由于太阳系中的每个天体在被称为重度轰击的时期（大约40亿年前）被小行星和彗星反复撞击，每个天体都应该至少收到一些有机分子。
However, these molecules tend to be destroyed by solar radiation on surfaces unprotected by atmospheres
然而，在没有大气层保护的表面上，这些分子往往被太阳辐射所破坏
Moreover, while these molecules might stay intact beneath the surface (as they evidently do on asteroids and comets), they probably cannot react with each other unless some kind of liquid or gas is available to move them about
此外，虽然这些分子可能在地表下保持完整（正如它们在小行星和彗星上显然所做的那样），但除非有某种液体或气体可供它们移动，否则它们可能无法相互反应。
Thus, if we limit our search to worlds on which organic molecules are likely to be involved in chemical reactions, we can probably rule out any world that lacks both an atmosphere and a surface or subsurface liquid medium, such as water
因此，如果我们把搜索范围限制在有机分子可能参与化学反应的世界上，我们也许可以排除任何既没有大气层又没有表面或地下液体介质的世界，如水
