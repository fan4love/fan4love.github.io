北美巨型动物的过度杀戮

Overkill of the North American Megafauna
北美巨型动物的过度杀戮

Thousands of years ago, in North America’s past, all of its megafauna—large mammals such as mammoths and giant bears—disappeared
几千年前，在北美的过去，所有的巨型动物–大型哺乳动物，如猛犸象和巨熊–都消失了。
One proposed explanation for this event is that when the first Americans migrated over from Asia, they hunted the megafauna to extinction
对这一事件提出的一个解释是，当第一批美国人从亚洲迁移过来时，他们将巨型动物猎杀到灭绝。
These people, known as the Clovis society after a site where their distinctive spear points were first found, would have been able to use this food source to expand their population and fill the continent rapidly
这些人因其独特的矛头首次被发现而被称为克洛维斯社会，他们能够利用这一食物来源扩大人口并迅速填满大陆。
Yet many scientists argue against this “Pleistocene overkill” hypothesis
然而，许多科学家反对这种 “更新世过度杀戮 “的假说
Modern humans have certainly been capable of such drastic effects on animals, but could ancient people with little more than stone spears similarly have caused the extinction of numerous species of animals?Thirty-five genera or groups of species (and many individual species) suffered extinction in North America around 11,000 B
现代人当然有能力对动物产生如此剧烈的影响，但除了石矛之外没有什么别的东西的古代人是否也能同样造成众多动物物种的灭绝？

Although the climate changed at the end of the Pleistocene, warming trends had happened before
虽然气候在更新世末期发生了变化，但变暖趋势在之前就已经发生了
A period of massive extinction of large mammals like that seen about 11,000 years ago had not occurred during the previous 400,000 years, despite these changes
尽管发生了这些变化，像大约1.1万年前那样的大型哺乳动物大规模灭绝的时期在之前的40万年里并没有发生。
The only apparently significant difference in the Americas 11,000 years ago was the presence of human hunters of these large mammals
1.1万年前的美洲唯一明显的区别是出现了人类对这些大型哺乳动物的狩猎者

研究人员以多种方式运行该模型，总是从加拿大阿尔伯塔省埃德蒙顿的100个人类人口开始，时间为11500年前

For example, geologist Larry Agenbroad has mapped the locations of dated Clovis sites alongside the distribution of dated sites where the remains of wooly mammoths have been found in both archaeological and purely paleontological contexts
例如，地质学家拉里-阿根布罗德（Larry Agenbroad）绘制了有日期的克洛维斯遗址的位置图，以及在考古和纯古生物学背景下发现长毛象遗骸的有日期遗址的分布图。

重要的是，尽管有几个遗址相当令人印象深刻，但确实没有什么考古学证据来支持它。
Writing in 1982, Martin himself admitted the paucity of evidence;for example, at that point, the remains of only 38 individual mammoths had been found at Clovis sites
马丁在1982年写道，他自己也承认了证据的匮乏；例如，当时在克洛维斯遗址只发现了38头个体猛犸象的遗骸。
In the years since, few additional mammoths have been added to the list;there are still fewer than 20 Clovis sites where the remains of one or more mammoths have been recovered, a minuscule proportion of the millions that necessarily would have had to have been slaughtered within the overkill scenario
在此后的几年里，很少有新的猛犸象被列入名单；目前仍有不到20个克洛维斯遗址发现了一头或多头猛犸象的遗骸，这与在过度杀戮的情况下必然要被屠杀的数百万猛犸象相比，只是一个很小的比例。

Though Martin claims the lack of evidence actually supports his model—the evidence is sparse because the spread of humans and the extinction of animals occurred so quickly—this argument seems weak
尽管马丁声称证据的缺乏实际上支持了他的模型–证据稀少是因为人类的传播和动物的灭绝发生得太快了–这一论点似乎很弱
And how could we ever disprove it?As archaeologist Donald Grayson points out, in other cases where extinction resulted from the quick spread of human hunters—for example, the extinction of the moa, the large flightless bird of New Zealand—archaeological evidence in the form of remains is abundant
正如考古学家唐纳德-格雷森（Donald Grayson）所指出的，在其他由于人类猎人的迅速传播而导致灭绝的案例中–例如，新西兰的大型不飞鸟–驼鸟的灭绝–遗骸形式的考古学证据非常丰富。
Grayson has also shown that the evidence is not so clear that all or even most of the large herbivores in late Pleistocene America became extinct after the appearance of Clovis
格雷森还表明，证据并不十分清楚，晚更新世美洲的所有或甚至大多数大型食草动物在克洛维斯出现后就灭绝了。
Of the 35 extinct genera, only 8 can be confidently assigned an extinction date of between 12,000 and 10,000 years ago
在35个灭绝的属中，只有8个可以有把握地将灭绝日期定在12000年至10000年之间
Many of the older genera, Grayson argues, may have succumbed before 12,000 B
格雷森认为，许多较早的属种，可能在公元前12000年以前就已经屈服了。
