Then the executioner began to scrutinize the criminals that had been kept in seclusion for days, his face steadfast and his rifle shined with splendor. Some watchers’ clothes were smeared and spewed at the terrible scene. The executioner sheathed his sword and shouted: “Next one.” The second was a sly cheater who obtained $100,000 through one solitary method. He solicited: “I fear the savor of sword. Kill me with a rifle, please.

Blood spurted out, like sprouting flower. The criminal was still sober and felt seared. The bullet singed his body and killed him without much snag. Unit 16. Misery of the Negro Slaves

第十六单元 黑人奴隶的苦难

400 years ago, many European colonists with supple hair went to Africa and subverted the aboriginal empires. After the subjection, local residents succumbed to the colonists. The conquerors found them stocky and submissive. They surmised that those tangible traits made them suitable for strenuous work. So they shipped those Negroes to America in order to supersede animal labor.

400年前，许多头发柔顺的欧洲殖民者前往非洲，颠覆了原住民帝国。征服之后，当地居民向殖民者屈服。征服者发现他们身材魁梧、顺从。他们推测，这些有形的特征使他们适合从事繁重的工作。所以他们把这些黑人运到美国，以取代动物劳动。

During the 1600s, from African plains to the summits, millions of Negroes tallying the requirements were tied together with strap. Their noses were transfixed and their faces tinted. Then they were jammed into stout ships teeming slaves thrust by sails and shipped to America. While in the ship, they fed on tepid tenuous porridge and stodgy corn. Stripped of all things, their head thumped the deck and their noses tingled while the ship waved.

在16世纪，从非洲平原到山顶，数以百万计的黑人在统计要求时被用皮带绑在一起。他们的鼻子被绑住，脸被染上颜色。然后，他们被塞进装满奴隶的坚固船只，被船帆推着运往美国。在船上时，他们以不温不火的稀粥和呆板的玉米为食。他们被剥夺了所有的东西，他们的头在甲板上砰砰作响，他们的鼻子在船的摇晃下刺痛。

Such bad conditions were not tentative, so they became torpid and tranquil as well as susceptible. Surplus people were thrown into the sea and were submerged by water. As the slavers kept tantalizing them, Even the most temperate man became sullen. Their touchy temperament caused much strife and many ships strayed. After they finally surmounted all the difficulties stunting them and subsisted until they reached America, they found themselves suffusing the slaver’s farms to which they were subsidiary.

这样恶劣的条件下，他们没有试探性，所以他们变得麻木不仁，安分守己，也很容易受到影响。多余的人被扔进海里，被水淹没了。由于奴隶主不断挑逗他们，即使是最有脾气的人也变得闷闷不乐。他们易怒的性情引起了许多纷争，许多船只迷失了方向。 在他们最终克服了所有阻碍他们的困难，并在到达美洲之前维持生计之后，他们发现自己在奴隶主的农场里充斥着他们的附属品。

Their stingy superintendents stipulated with them that, their job was temporal and would soon be supplanted by more succinct work. It’s their tact however. Everyday they ate their sustenance when the bell tolled. It was tacit in the synopsis of the contact that they can never be free. Their thrift and torment transcended all that in human history.

他们吝啬的主管对他们规定，他们的工作是暂时的，很快会被更简洁的工作所取代。但这是他们的策略。每天，当钟声响起时，他们就会吃上自己的养料。在接触的梗概中默示，他们永远不可能获得自由。他们的节俭和煎熬超越了人类历史上的一切。

When their deeds transpired, the colonists’ reputation got tarnished. International organizations sublimated the topic of fighting against slavery and began to tackle the problem.
