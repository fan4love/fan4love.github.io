Evidence of the Earliest Writing
最早的文字证据

Although literacy appeared independently in several parts of the prehistoric world, the earliest evidence of writing is the cuneiform Sumerian script on the clay tablets of ancient Mesopotamia, which, archaeological detective work has revealed, had its origins in the accounting practices of commercial activity
尽管识字在史前世界的几个地方独立出现，但最早的文字证据是古代美索不达米亚的泥板上的楔形苏美尔文字，考古探测工作显示，这种文字起源于商业活动的会计惯例。
Researchers demonstrated that preliterate people, to keep track of the goods they produced and exchanged, created a system of accounting using clay tokens as symbolic representations of their products
研究人员证明，识字前的人们为了记录他们生产和交换的货物，创造了一种使用粘土代币作为其产品的象征性代表的会计系统。
Over many thousands of years, the symbols evolved through several stages of abstraction until they became wedge- shaped (cuneiform) signs on clay tablets, recognizable as writing
几千年来，这些符号经过几个阶段的抽象化演变，直到它们成为泥板上的楔形符号（楔形文字），可以被识别为文字。

The evolution of the symbolism is reflected in the archaeological record first of all by the increasing complexity of the tokens themselves
象征主义的演变首先反映在考古记录中，就是令牌本身的复杂性不断增加。

The earlier, plain tokens were counters for agricultural products, whereas the complex ones stood for finished products, such as bread, oil, perfume, wool, and rope, and for items produced in workshops, such as metal, bracelets, types of cloth, garments, mats, pieces of furniture, tools, and a variety of stone and pottery vessels
早期的普通代币是农产品的计数器，而复杂的代币则代表成品，如面包、油、香水、羊毛和绳子，以及工场生产的物品，如金属、手镯、各类布匹、服装、垫子、家具、工具和各种石器和陶器。
The signs marked on clay tablets likewise evolved from simple wedges, circles, ovals, and triangles based on the plain tokens to pictographs derived from the complex tokens
泥板上标记的符号也同样从简单的楔形、圆形、椭圆形和三角形发展到了从复杂符号衍生出来的象形文字。

Before this evidence came to light, the inventors of writing were assumed by researchers to have been an intellectual elite
在这一证据出现之前，研究人员认为文字的发明者是一个知识精英。
Some, for example, hypothesized that writing emerged when members of the priestly caste agreed among themselves on written signs
例如，有些人假设，当祭司种姓的成员之间就书面符号达成一致时，就出现了文字。
But the association of the plain tokens with the first farmers and of the complex tokens with the first artisans—and the fact that the token-and-envelope accounting system invariably represented only small-scale transactions—testifies to the relatively modest social status of the creators of writing
但是，将普通代币与第一批农民联系起来，将复杂的代币与第一批工匠联系起来，以及代币和信封会计系统始终只代表小规模的交易这一事实，证明了文字创造者相对温和的社会地位。

And not only of literacy, but numeracy (the representation of quantitative concepts) as well
而且不仅是识字，还有计算能力（数量概念的表述）。
The evidence of the tokens provides further confirmation that mathematics originated in people’s desire to keep records of flocks and other goods

What was the social status of the anonymous accountants who produced this breakthrough? The immense volume of clay tablets unearthed in the ruins of the Sumerian temples where the accounts were kept suggests a social differentiation within the scribal class, with a virtual army of lower-ranking tabulators performing the monotonous job of tallying commodities

We can only speculate as to how high or low the inventors of true numerals were in the scribal hierarchy, but it stands to reason that this laborsaving innovation would have been the brainchild of the lower-ranking types whose drudgery is eased
