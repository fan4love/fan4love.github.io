木材的商业化

The Commercialization of Lumber
木材的商业化
In nineteenth-century America, practically everything that was built involved wood
在十九世纪的美国，几乎所有的建筑都涉及到木材。
Pine was especially attractive for building purposes
松树在建筑方面特别有吸引力 松树在建筑方面特别具有吸引力 松树在建筑方面尤其具有吸引力 松树在建筑方面尤其有吸引力
It is durable and strong, yet soft enough to be easily worked with even the simplest of hand tools
它耐用而坚固，但又足够柔软，甚至可以用最简单的手工工具轻松加工。
It also floats nicely on water, which allowed it to be transported to distant markets across the nation
它还能很好地漂浮在水面上，这使得它能够被运往全国各地的遥远市场。
The central and northern reaches of the Great Lakes states—Michigan, Wisconsin, and Minnesota—all contained extensive pine forests as well as many large rivers for floating logs into the Great Lakes, from where they were transported nationwide
大湖区各州的中部和北部–密歇根州、威斯康星州和明尼苏达州–都有广泛的松树林以及许多大河，可以将原木漂流到大湖区，从那里运往全国各地。

By 1860, the settlement of the American West along with timber shortages in the East converged with ever-widening impact on the pine forests of the Great Lakes states
到1860年，美国西部的定居和东部的木材短缺对大湖区各州的松树林产生了越来越广泛的影响。
Over the next 30 years, lumbering became a full-fledged enterprise in Michigan, Wisconsin, and Minnesota
在接下来的30年里，伐木业在密歇根州、威斯康星州和明尼苏达州成为一个全面的企业。
Newly formed lumbering corporations bought up huge tracts of pineland and set about systematically cutting the trees
新成立的伐木公司买下了大片的松树地，开始有计划地砍伐树木。
Both the colonists and the later industrialists saw timber as a commodity, but the latter group adopted a far more thorough and calculating approach to removing trees
殖民者和后来的工业家都把木材看作是一种商品，但后者采取了一种更彻底和更有计划的方法来移除树木。
In this sense, what happened between 1860 and 1890 represented a significant break with the past
在这个意义上，1860年至1890年间发生的事情代表了与过去的重大决裂
No longer were farmers in search of extra income the main source for shingles, firewood, and other wood products
寻求额外收入的农民不再是瓦片、木柴和其他木制品的主要来源。
By the 1870s, farmers and city dwellers alike purchased forest products from large manufacturing companies located in the Great Lakes states rather than chopping wood themselves or buying it locally
到19世纪70年代，农民和城市居民都从位于大湖区各州的大型制造公司购买森林产品，而不是自己砍伐木材或在当地购买。

The commercialization of lumbering was in part the product of technological change
伐木业的商业化在一定程度上是技术变革的产物。
The early, thick saw blades tended to waste a large quantity of wood, with perhaps as much as a third of the log left behind on the floor as sawdust or scrap
早期厚重的锯片往往会浪费大量的木材，可能有多达三分之一的原木作为锯末或废料留在地板上。
In the 1870s, however, the British-invented band saw, with its thinner blade, became standard issue in the Great Lakes states’ lumber factories
然而，在19世纪70年代，英国发明的带锯及其较薄的锯片，成为大湖区各州木材厂的标准配置。
Meanwhile, the rise of steam-powered mills streamlined production by allowing for the more efficient, centralized, and continuous cutting of lumber
同时，蒸汽动力工厂的兴起使生产更加合理，使木材的切割更加高效、集中和连续。
Steam helped to automate a variety of tasks, from cutting to the carrying away of waste
蒸汽帮助实现了各种任务的自动化，从切割到运走废物。
Mills also employed steam to heat log ponds, preventing them from freezing and making possible year-round lumber production
磨坊还利用蒸汽来加热原木池，防止其结冰，使全年的木材生产成为可能。

For industrial lumbering to succeed, a way had to be found to neutralize the effects of the seasons on production
为了使工业化伐木获得成功，必须找到一种方法来中和季节对生产的影响。
Traditionally, cutting took place in the winter, when snow and ice made it easier to drag logs on sleds or sleighs to the banks of streams
传统上，砍伐是在冬季进行的，当时冰雪使人们更容易用雪橇或雪橇将原木拖到溪流的岸边。
Once the streams and lakes thawed, workers rafted the logs to mills, where they were cut into lumber in the summer
一旦溪流和湖泊解冻，工人们就用筏子把原木运到工厂，在夏天将它们切割成木材。
If nature did not cooperate—if the winter proved dry and warm, if the spring thaw was delayed—production would suffer
如果大自然不配合–如果冬天被证明是干燥和温暖的，如果春天的解冻被推迟–生产将受到影响。
To counter the effects of climate on lumber production, loggers experimented with a variety of techniques for transporting trees out of the woods
为了应对气候对木材生产的影响，伐木者尝试了各种将树木运出森林的技术
In the 1870s, loggers in the Great Lakes states began sprinkling water on sleigh roads, giving them an artificial ice coating to facilitate travel
19世纪70年代，大湖区各州的伐木工人开始在雪橇道路上洒水，给它们涂上一层人工冰层，以方便旅行。
The ice reduced the friction and allowed workers to move larger and heavier loads
冰块减少了摩擦，使工人能够移动更大和更重的货物

But all the sprinkling in the world would not save a logger from the threat of a warm winter
但是，世界上所有的洒水都不能拯救一个伐木工人免受暖冬的威胁。
Without snow the sleigh roads turned to mud
没有雪，雪橇的道路变成了泥泞 没有雪，雪橇路就变成了泥巴路 没有雪，雪橇路就变成了泥巴 没有雪，雪橇的道路变成了泥泞。
In the 1870s, a set of snowless winters left lumber companies to ponder ways of liberating themselves from the seasons
19世纪70年代，一系列无雪的冬天让木材公司开始思考如何将自己从季节中解放出来。
