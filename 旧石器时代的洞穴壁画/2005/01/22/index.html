旧石器时代的洞穴壁画

Paleolithic Cave Paintings
旧石器时代的洞穴壁画

In any investigation of the origins of art, attention focuses on the cave paintings created in Europe during the Paleolithic era (c
在对艺术起源的任何调查中，人们的注意力都集中在旧石器时代在欧洲创造的洞穴壁画上（C
40,000-10,000 years ago) such as those depicting bulls and other animals in the Lascaux cave in France
40,000-10,000年前），如法国拉斯科洞穴中描绘公牛和其他动物的画像。
Accepting that they are the best preserved and most visible signs of what was a global creative explosion, how do we start to explain their appearance? Instinctively, we may want to update the earliest human artists by assuming that they painted for the sheer joy of painting
接受他们是当时全球创造性爆发的最好的保存和最明显的标志，我们如何开始解释他们的外观？本能地，我们可能想更新最早的人类艺术家，假设他们是为了纯粹的绘画乐趣而作画。
The philosophers of Classical Greece recognized it as a defining trait of humans to “delight in works of imitation”—to enjoy the very act and triumph of representation
古典希腊的哲学家们认为，”以模仿的作品为乐 “是人类的一个决定性特征–享受表现的行为和胜利。
If we were close to a real lion or snake, we might feel frightened
如果我们接近真正的狮子或蛇，我们可能会感到害怕
But a well- executed picture of a lion or snake will give us pleasure
但一幅表现良好的狮子或蛇的图片会给我们带来快乐
Why suppose that our Paleolithic ancestors were any different?
This simple acceptance of art for art’s sake has a certain appeal
为什么认为我们的旧石器时代的祖先有什么不同？ 这种为艺术而艺术的简单接受有一定的吸引力
To think of Lascaux as a gallery allows it to be a sort of special viewing place where the handiwork of accomplished artists might be displayed
将拉斯科视为一个画廊，使其成为一种特殊的观赏场所，有成就的艺术家的手工作品可能会在这里展出。
Plausibly, daily existence in parts of Paleolithic Europe may not have been so hard, with an abundance of ready food and therefore the leisure time for art
可以说，旧石器时代欧洲部分地区的日常生活可能并不那么艰苦，有大量的现成食物，因此有闲暇时间进行艺术创作。
The problems with this explanation, however, are various
然而，这种解释的问题是多方面的 但是，这种解释的问题是多方面的 然而，这种解释的问题有很多 然而，这种解释的问题是多种多样的
In the first place, the proliferation of archaeological discoveries—and this includes some of the world’s innumerable rock art sites that cannot be dated—has served to emphasize a remarkably limited repertoire of subjects
首先，考古发现的激增–这包括世界上一些无法确定年代的岩石艺术遗址–有助于强调一个非常有限的主题种类。
The images that recur are those of animals
反复出现的图像是那些动物的图像 重复出现的图像是那些动物的图像 反复出现的形象是那些动物的形象 反复出现的图像是那些动物的图像。
Human figures are unusual, and when they do make an appearance, they are rarely done with the same attention to form accorded to the animals
人类的形象是不寻常的，当他们出现时，他们很少以对动物的形式给予同样的关注来完成。
If Paleolithic artists were simply seeking to represent the beauty of the world around them, would they not have left a far greater range of pictures—of trees, flowers, of the Sun and the stars?
A further question to the theory of art for art’s sake is posed by the high incidence of Paleolithic images that appear not to be imitative of any reality whatsoever
如果旧石器时代的艺术家只是为了表现他们周围世界的美，他们难道不会留下更多的图片–树木、花朵、太阳和星星？ 对为艺术而艺术的理论提出的另一个问题是，旧石器时代的大量图像似乎不是对任何现实的模仿。
These are geometrical shapes or patterns consisting of dots or lines
这些是由点或线组成的几何形状或图案
Such marks may be found isolated or repeated over a particular surface but also scattered across more recognizable forms
这种标记可以在一个特定的表面上发现孤立的或重复的，但也可以散布在更多可识别的形式上。
A good example of this may be seen in the geologically spectacular grotto of Pêche Merle, in the Lot region of France
这方面的一个很好的例子是位于法国洛特地区的佩什-梅尔（Pêche Merle）地质学上的壮观石窟。
Here we encounter some favorite animals from the Paleolithic repertoire—a pair of stout-bellied horses
在这里，我们遇到了旧石器时代的一些喜爱的动物–一对粗壮的腹马
But over and around the horses’ outlines are multiple dark spots, daubed in disregard for the otherwise naturalistic representation of animals
但在马的轮廓上方和周围有多个黑点，这些黑点是无视动物的自然表现而涂抹的。
What does such patterning imitate? There is also the factor of location
这种模式化模仿的是什么？还有一个因素是地点
The caves of Lascaux might conceivably qualify as underground galleries, but many other paintings have been found in recesses totally unsuitable for any kind of viewing—tight nooks and crannies that must have been awkward even for the artists to penetrate, let alone for anyone else wanting to see the art
拉斯科洞穴可以说是地下画廊，但其他许多画作都是在完全不适合观看的凹处发现的–狭小的角落和缝隙，连艺术家都很难进入，更不用说其他想看艺术的人了。

Finally, we may doubt the notion that the Upper Paleolithic period was a paradise in which food came readily, leaving humans ample time to amuse themselves with art
最后，我们可能会怀疑旧石器时代上部是一个天堂，食物来的很容易，让人类有充足的时间用艺术来消遣
For Europe it was still the Ice Age
对欧洲来说，当时仍然是冰河时期 对欧洲来说，当时仍然是冰河时代 对欧洲来说，当时仍是冰河时代 对欧洲来说，当时仍是冰河时期
An estimate of the basic level of sustenance then necessary for human survival has been judged at 2200 calories per day
据估计，当时人类生存所需的基本营养水平为每天2200卡路里。
This consideration, combined with the stark emphasis upon animals in the cave art, has persuaded some archaeologists that the primary motive behind Paleolithic images must lie with the primary activity of Paleolithic people: hunting
这种考虑，再加上洞穴艺术中对动物的明显强调，使一些考古学家相信，旧石器时代图像背后的主要动机必须是旧石器时代人的主要活动：打猎

Hunting is a skill
狩猎是一种技能
Tracking, stalking, chasing, and killing the prey are difficult, sometimes dangerous activities
追踪、跟踪、追赶和杀死猎物是困难的，有时是危险的活动。
What if the process could be made easier—by art? In the early decades of the twentieth century, Abbé Henri Breuil argued that the cave paintings were all about “sympathetic magic
如果这个过程可以通过艺术变得更容易呢？在二十世纪初的几十年里，亨利-布雷尔神父（Abbé Henri Breuil）认为，洞穴壁画是关于 “同情的魔法”。
” The artists strived diligently to make their animal images evocative and realistic because they were attempting to capture the spirit of their prey
“ 艺术家们努力使他们的动物形象令人回味和逼真，因为他们试图捕捉猎物的精神。
What could have prompted their studious attention to making such naturalistic, recognizable images? According to Breuil, the artists may have believed that if a hunter were able to make a true likeness of some animal, then that animal was virtually trapped
是什么促使他们专注于制作这种自然的、可识别的图像？根据Breuil的说法，艺术家们可能认为，如果一个猎人能够做出某种动物的真实肖像，那么这种动物实际上就被困住了。
Images, therefore, may have had the magical capacity to confer success or luck in the hunt
因此，图像可能具有神奇的能力，可以在狩猎中获得成功或幸运。
