雨林的土壤

Rain Forest Soils
雨林的土壤

On viewing the lush plant growth of a tropical rain forest, most people would conclude that the soil beneath it is rich in nutrients
当看到热带雨林中茂盛的植物生长时，大多数人都会得出结论，它下面的土壤含有丰富的营养物质。
However, although rain forest soils are highly variable, they have in common the fact that abundant rainfall washes mineral nutrients out of them and into streams
然而，尽管热带雨林的土壤变化很大，但它们有一个共同的事实，即大量的降雨将矿物养分从土壤中冲出，进入溪流。
This process is known as leaching
这一过程被称为浸出 这个过程被称为浸出 这一过程被称为沥滤 这个过程被称为沥滤
Because of rain leaching, most tropical rain forest soils have low to very low mineral nutrient content, in dramatic contrast to mineral-rich grassland soils
由于雨水冲刷，大多数热带雨林土壤的矿物质养分含量较低或非常低，与矿物质丰富的草原土壤形成鲜明对比。
Tropical forest soils also often contain particular types of clays that, unlike the mineral-binding clays of temperate forest soils, do not bind mineral ions well
热带森林土壤还经常含有特殊类型的粘土，与温带森林土壤的矿物结合粘土不同，它们不能很好地结合矿物离子。
Aluminum is the dominant cation (positively charged ion) present in tropical soils; but plants do not require this element, and it is moderately toxic to a wide range of plants
铝是热带土壤中最主要的阳离子（带正电的离子）；但植物不需要这种元素，而且它对多种植物有中等毒性。
Aluminum also reduces the availability of phosphorus, an element in high demand by plants
铝还会减少磷的供应，而磷是植物高度需求的元素。

High moisture and temperatures speed the growth of soil microbes that decompose organic compounds, so tropical soils typically contain far lower amounts of organic materials (humus) than do other forest or grassland soils
高湿度和高温度加速了分解有机化合物的土壤微生物的生长，因此热带土壤中的有机物质（腐殖质）含量通常远远低于其他森林或草原土壤。
Because organic compounds help loosen compact clay soils, hold water, and bind mineral nutrients, the relative lack of organic materials in tropical soils is deleterious to plants
由于有机化合物有助于疏松紧实的粘土，保持水分，并结合矿物养分，所以热带土壤中相对缺乏的有机材料对植物是有害的。
Plant roots cannot penetrate far into hard clay soils, and during dry periods, the soil cannot hold enough water to supply plant needs
植物的根系不能深入到坚硬的粘土中，在干旱时期，土壤不能保持足够的水分来供应植物的需要。
Because the concentration of dark-colored organic materials is low in tropical soils, they are often colored red or yellow by the presence of iron, aluminum: and manganese oxides; when dry, these soils become rock hard
由于热带土壤中深色有机物的浓度很低，它们通常因铁、铝和锰氧化物的存在而被染成红色或黄色；干燥时，这些土壤变得坚硬如石。
The famous Cambodian temples of Angkor Wat, which have survived for many centuries, were constructed from blocks of such hard rain forest soils
保存了许多世纪的柬埔寨著名的吴哥窟寺庙，就是用这种坚硬的雨林土壤块建造的。

Given such poor soils, how can lush tropical forests exist? The answer is that the forest’s minerals are held in its living biomass—the trees and other plants and the animals
在如此贫瘠的土壤中，茂盛的热带森林如何存在？答案是，森林的矿物质存在于其生物质中–树木和其他植物以及动物。
In contrast to grasslands, where a large proportion of plant biomass is produced underground, that of tropical forests is nearly all aboveground
草原上的植物生物量有很大一部分是在地下产生的，而热带森林的生物量几乎都是在地上产生的。
Dead leaves, branches, and other plant parts, as well as the wastes and bodies of rain forest animals, barely reach the forest floor before they are rapidly decayed by abundant decomposers—bacterial and fungal
枯叶、树枝和其他植物部分，以及雨林动物的废物和尸体，在被丰富的分解者–细菌和真菌–迅速腐烂之前，几乎没有到达森林地面。
Minerals released by decay are quickly absorbed by multitudinous shallow, fine tree feeder roots and stored in plant tissues
腐烂所释放的矿物质迅速被众多的浅层细树根吸收，并储存在植物组织中。
Many tropical rain forest plants (like those in other forests) have mycorrhizal (fungus-root) partners whose delicate hyphae spread through great volumes of soil, from which they release and absorb minerals and ferry them back to the host plant in exchange for needed organic compounds
许多热带雨林植物（像其他森林中的植物一样）都有菌根（真菌-根）伙伴，其纤细的菌丝在大量的土壤中蔓延，它们从土壤中释放和吸收矿物质，并将它们运回宿主植物，以换取所需的有机化合物。
The fungal hyphae are able to absorb phosphorus that plant roots could not themselves obtain from the very dilute soil solutions, and fungal hyphae can transfer mineral nutrients from one forest plant to another
真菌菌丝能够吸收植物根系无法自己从非常稀薄的土壤溶液中获得的磷，而且真菌菌丝可以将矿物营养物质从一种森林植物转移到另一种森林植物中。
Consequently, tropical rain forests typically have what are known as closed nutrient systems, in which minerals are handed off from one organism to another with little leaking through to the soil
因此，热带雨林通常具有所谓的封闭式营养系统，其中矿物质从一个生物体转移到另一个生物体，很少渗漏到土壤中。
When mineral nutrients do not spend much time in the soil, they cannot be leached into streams
当矿物养分在土壤中停留的时间不长时，它们就不能被浸入溪流。
Closed nutrient systems have evolved in response to the leaching effects of heavy tropical rainfall
封闭式营养系统是为了应对热带强降雨的沥滤效应而演变的
Evidence for this conclusion is that nutrient systems are more open in the richest tropical soils and tightest in the poorest soils
这一结论的证据是，养分系统在最丰富的热带土壤中更为开放，而在最贫穷的土壤中最为紧密。

The growth of organisms is dependent on the availability of nutrients, none of which is more important than nitrogen
生物体的生长取决于营养物质的供应，其中最重要的莫过于氮。
Although there is an abundant supply of nitrogen in Earth’s atmosphere, it cannot be absorbed by plants unless it is “fixed,” or combined chemically with other elements to form nitrogen compounds
尽管地球大气中存在丰富的氮供应，但它不能被植物吸收，除非它被 “固定”，或与其他元素化学结合，形成氮化合物。
Nitrogen-fixing bacteria help tropical rain forest plants cope with the poor soils there by supplying them with needed nitrogen
固氮细菌通过为热带雨林植物提供所需的氮，帮助它们应对那里贫瘠的土壤
Many species of tropical rain forest trees belong to the legume family, which is known for associations of nitrogen-fixing bacteria within root nodules
许多热带雨林树种属于豆科植物，因根瘤内有固氮细菌而闻名。
Also, cycads (a type of tropical plant that resembles a palm tree) produce special aboveground roots that harbor nitrogen-fixing cyanobacteria
此外，苏铁（一种类似于棕榈树的热带植物）产生特殊的地上根系，孕育着固氮的蓝藻。
By growing above the ground, the roots are exposed to sunlight, which the cyanobacteria require for growth
通过在地面上生长，根部暴露在阳光下，而蓝细菌的生长需要阳光。
Nitrogen fixation by free-living bacteria in tropical soils is also beneficial
热带土壤中自由生活的细菌的固氮作用也是有益的
