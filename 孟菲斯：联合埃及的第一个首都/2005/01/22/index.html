孟菲斯：联合埃及的第一个首都

Memphis: United Egypt’s First Capital
孟菲斯：联合埃及的第一个首都

The city of Memphis, located on the Nile near the modern city of Cairo, was founded around 3100 B
孟菲斯城位于尼罗河上，靠近现代城市开罗，大约在公元前3100年建立。

The choice of Memphis by Egypt’s first kings reflects the site’s strategic importance
埃及的第一个国王选择孟菲斯，反映了该地的战略重要性
First, and most obvious, the apex of the Nile River delta was a politically opportune location for the state’s administrative center, standing between the united lands of Upper and Lower Egypt and offering ready access to both parts of the country
首先，也是最明显的一点，尼罗河三角洲的顶点是国家行政中心的一个政治上的有利位置，它位于上埃及和下埃及的统一土地之间，可以随时进入该国的两个地区。

Only a city within easy reach of both the Nile valley to the south and the more spread out, difficult terrain to the north could provide the necessary political control that the rulers of early dynastic Egypt (roughly 3000-2600 B
只有在南边的尼罗河流域和北边更分散、更困难的地形中都能方便地找到一个城市，才能提供必要的政治控制，使埃及早期王朝的统治者（大约在公元前3000-2600年）能够实现。

The region of Memphis must have also served as an important node for transport and communications, even before the unification of Egypt
孟菲斯地区肯定也是一个重要的交通和通信节点，甚至在埃及统一之前也是如此。
The region probably acted as a conduit for much, if not all, of the river-based trade between northern and southern Egypt
该地区可能是埃及北部和南部之间大部分（如果不是全部）基于河流的贸易的通道。
Moreover, commodities (such as wine, precious oils, and metals) imported from the Near East by the royal courts of predynastic Upper Egypt would have been channeled through the Memphis region on their way south
此外，上古时期上埃及王室从近东进口的商品（如葡萄酒、贵重油料和金属），在南下的路上会经过孟菲斯地区。
In short, therefore, the site of Memphis offered the rulers of the Early Dynastic Period an ideal location for controlling internal trade within their realm, an essential requirement for a state-directed economy that depended on the movement of goods
因此，简而言之，孟菲斯为早期王朝时期的统治者提供了一个控制其境内贸易的理想地点，这对于依赖货物流动的国家指导型经济来说是一个基本要求。

Equally important for the national administration was the ability to control communications within Egypt
对国家行政当局来说，同样重要的是控制埃及境内的通信的能力。
The Nile provided the easiest and quickest artery of communication and the national capital was, again, ideally located in this respect
尼罗河提供了最方便快捷的交通动脉，而国家首都在这方面的位置也非常理想。
Recent geological surveys of the Memphis region have revealed much about its topography in ancient times
最近对孟菲斯地区的地质调查揭示了其古代地形的许多情况
It appears that the location of Memphis may have been even more advantageous for controlling trade, transport, and communications than was previously appreciated
看来，孟菲斯的位置对于控制贸易、运输和通信来说，可能比以前所了解的更加有利。
Surveys and drill cores have shown that the level of the Nile floodplain has steadily risen over the last five millenniums
勘测和钻芯显示，尼罗河洪泛区的水平在过去五千年中一直稳步上升
When the floodplain was much lower, as it would have been in predynastic and early dynastic times, the outwash fans (fan-shaped deposits of sediments) of various wadis (stream-beds or channels that carry water only during rainy periods) would have been much more prominent features on the east bank
当洪泛区低得多时，就像在王朝前和王朝早期那样，各种瓦地（只在雨季运水的河床或渠道）的冲刷扇（扇形沉积物）会成为东岸更突出的特征。
The fan associated with the Wadi Hof extended a significant way into the Nile floodplain, forming a constriction in the vicinity of Memphis
与瓦迪-霍夫有关的扇形物延伸到尼罗河洪泛区，在孟菲斯附近形成了一个限制。
The valley may have narrowed at this point to a mere three kilometers, making it the ideal place for controlling river traffic
山谷在这一点上可能已经缩小到只有三公里，使其成为控制河流交通的理想场所

Furthermore, the Memphis region seems to have been favorably located for the control not only of river-based trade but also of desert trade routes
此外，孟菲斯地区似乎处于有利的位置，不仅可以控制基于河流的贸易，而且可以控制沙漠贸易路线。
The two outwash fans in the area gave access to the extensive wadi systems of the eastern desert
该地区的两个冲积扇使人们可以进入东部沙漠的广泛的瓦迪系统。
In predynastic times, the Wadi Digla may have served as a trade route between the Memphis region and the Near East, to judge from the unusual concentration of foreign artifacts found in the predynastic settlement of Maadi
在公元前时代，瓦迪迪格拉可能是孟菲斯地区和近东之间的一条贸易路线，这可以从马迪的公元前定居点中发现的不寻常的外国文物中判断出来。
Access to, and control of, trade routes between Egypt and the Near East seems to have been a preoccupation of Egypt’s rulers during the period of state formation
进入并控制埃及和近东之间的贸易路线，似乎是埃及统治者在国家形成时期的一个首要任务。
The desire to monopolize foreign trade may have been one of the primary factors behind the political unification of Egypt
垄断对外贸易的愿望可能是埃及政治统一的主要因素之一。
The foundation of the national capital at the junction of an important trade route with the Nile valley is not likely to have been accidental
国家首都建立在与尼罗河流域的重要贸易路线的交界处，这不可能是偶然的。
Moreover, the Wadis Hof and Digla provided the Memphis region with accessible desert pasturage
此外，Wadis Hof和Digla为孟菲斯地区提供了方便的沙漠牧草
As was the case with the cities of Hierakonpolis and Elkab, the combination within the same area of both desert pasturage and alluvial arable land (land suitable for growing crops) was a particularly attractive one for early settlement; this combination no doubt contributed to the prosperity of the Memphis region from early predynastic times
正如希拉孔波利斯和艾尔卡布城市的情况一样，在同一地区同时拥有沙漠牧场和冲积耕地（适合种植农作物的土地）对早期定居者来说是特别有吸引力的；这种组合无疑有助于孟菲斯地区从王朝前早期开始的繁荣。
