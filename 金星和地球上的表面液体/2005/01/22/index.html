金星和地球上的表面液体

Surface Fluids on Venus and Earth
金星和地球上的表面液体

A fluid is a substance, such as a liquid or gas, in which the component particles (usually molecules) can move past one another
流体是一种物质，如液体或气体，其中的组成颗粒（通常是分子）可以相互移动。
Fluids flow easily and conform to the shape of their containers
液体容易流动并符合其容器的形状
The geologic processes related to the movement of fluids on a planet’s surface can completely resurface a planet many times
与行星表面流体运动有关的地质过程可以使一个行星完全重新浮出水面许多次
These processes derive their energy from the Sun and the gravitational forces of the planet itself
这些过程从太阳和行星本身的引力中获得能量
As these fluids interact with surface materials, they move particles about or react chemically with them to modify or produce materials
当这些液体与表面材料相互作用时，它们会使颗粒移动或与它们发生化学反应，从而改变或产生材料。
On a solid planet with a hydrosphere and an atmosphere, only a tiny fraction of the planetary mass flows as surface fluids
在一个有水圈和大气的固体行星上，只有极小部分的行星质量以表面流体的形式流动。
Yet the movements of these fluids can drastically alter a planet
然而，这些流体的运动可以极大地改变一个星球。
Consider Venus and Earth, both terrestrial planets with atmospheres
考虑到金星和地球，这两颗陆地行星都有大气层

Venus and Earth are commonly regarded as twin planets but not identical twins
金星和地球通常被认为是双胞胎行星，但不是同卵双胞胎。
They are about the same size, are composed of roughly the same mix of materials, and may have been comparably endowed at their beginning with carbon dioxide and water
它们的大小差不多，由大致相同的材料组合组成，并且在开始时可能具有相当的二氧化碳和水。
However, the twins evolved differently largely because of differences in their distance from the Sun
然而，这对双胞胎的进化方式不同，主要是因为它们与太阳的距离不同。
With a significant amount of internal heat, Venus may continue to be geologically active with volcanoes, rifting, and folding
由于有大量的内部热量，金星可能会继续在地质上活跃，出现火山、断裂和褶皱等现象。
However, it lacks any sign of a hydrologic system (water circulation and distribution): there are no streams, lakes oceans or glaciers
然而，它缺乏任何水文系统（水循环和分配）的迹象：没有溪流、湖泊、海洋或冰川。
Space probes suggest that Venus may have started with as much water as Earth, but it was unable to keep its water in liquid form
空间探测器表明，金星开始时可能有和地球一样多的水，但它无法将其水保持在液态形式。
Because Venus receives more heat from the Sun, water released from the interior evaporated and rose to the upper atmosphere where the Sun’s ultraviolet rays broke the molecules apart
由于金星从太阳接收到更多的热量，从内部释放的水蒸发并上升到大气层上部，在那里太阳的紫外线将分子分解开来
Much of the freed hydrogen escaped into space, and Venus lost its water
大部分被释放的氢气逃到了太空中，而金星失去了它的水。
Without water, Venus became less and less like Earth and kept an atmosphere filled with carbon dioxide
没有水，金星变得越来越不像地球，并保持着充满二氧化碳的大气层
The carbon dioxide acts as a blanket, creating an intense greenhouse effect and driving surface temperatures high enough to melt lead and to prohibit the formation of carbonate minerals
二氧化碳就像一张毯子，产生强烈的温室效应，使表面温度高到足以融化铅，并禁止碳酸盐矿物的形成。
Volcanoes continually vented more carbon dioxide into the atmosphere
火山不断地将更多的二氧化碳排放到大气中
On Earth, liquid water removes carbon dioxide from the atmosphere and combines it with calcium, from rock weathering, to form carbonate sedimentary rocks
在地球上，液态水从大气中带走二氧化碳，并与岩石风化产生的钙结合，形成碳酸盐沉积岩。
Without liquid water to remove carbon from the atmosphere, the level of carbon dioxide in the atmosphere of Venus remains high
如果没有液态水来清除大气中的碳，金星大气中的二氧化碳水平仍然很高

Like Venus, Earth is large enough to be geologically active and for its gravitational field to hold an atmosphere
像金星一样，地球大到足以让地质活动活跃，其引力场足以容纳大气。
Unlike Venus, it is just the right distance from the Sun so that temperature ranges allow water to exist as a liquid, a solid, and a gas
与金星不同，它与太阳的距离恰到好处，因此温度范围允许水以液体、固体和气体的形式存在。
Water is thus extremely mobile and moves rapidly over the planet in a continuous hydrologic cycle
因此，水具有极强的流动性，在连续的水文循环中在地球上迅速移动
Heated by the Sun, the water moves in great cycles from the oceans to the atmosphere, over the landscape in river systems, and ultimately back to the oceans
在太阳的加热下，水在巨大的循环中从海洋流向大气层，在河流系统中穿过景观，并最终回到海洋中。
As a result, Earth’s surface has been continually changed and eroded into delicate systems of river valleys - a remarkable contrast to the surfaces of other planetary bodies where impact craters dominate
因此，地球的表面被不断地改变，并被侵蚀成精致的河谷系统–这与其他行星体的表面形成了显著的对比，这些行星体的撞击坑占主导地位。
Few areas on Earth have been untouched by flowing water
地球上很少有地区没有被流动的水触动过 地球上很少有地区没有被流动的水影响过 地球上很少有地区没有被流水冲刷过 地球上很少有地区没有被流动的水所影响
As a result, river valleys are the dominant feature of its landscape
因此，河谷是其景观的主要特征。
Similarly, wind action has scoured fine particles away from large areas, depositing them elsewhere as vast sand seas dominated by dunes or in sheets of loess (fine-grained soil deposits)
同样，风的作用将细小的颗粒从大片地区吹走，在其他地方沉积成以沙丘为主的巨大沙海或成片的黄土（细粒土壤沉积）。
These fluid movements are caused by gravity flow systems energized by heat from the Sun
这些流体运动是由太阳的热量激发的重力流系统引起的
Other geologic changes occur when the gases in the atmosphere or water react with rocks at the surface to form new chemical compounds with different properties
当大气中的气体或水与地表的岩石发生反应，形成具有不同性质的新化合物时，就会发生其他地质变化。
An important example of this process was the removal of most of Earths carbon dioxide from its atmosphere to form carbonate rocks
这一过程的一个重要例子是将地球上的大部分二氧化碳从大气中移除，形成碳酸盐岩石。
However, if Earth were a little closer to the Sun, its oceans would evaporate; if it were farther from the Sun, the oceans would freeze solid
然而，如果地球离太阳再近一点，它的海洋就会蒸发；如果它离太阳再远一点，海洋就会冻结成固体。
Because liquid water was present, self-replicating molecules of carbon, hydrogen, and oxygen developed life early in Earth’s history and have radically modified its surface, blanketing huge parts of the continents with greenery
由于液态水的存在，碳、氢和氧的自我复制分子在地球历史的早期发展了生命，并从根本上改变了地球的表面，使大陆的大部分地区被绿色植物所覆盖。
Life thrives on this planet, and it helped create the planet’s oxygen- and nitrogen-rich atmosphere and moderate temperatures
生命在这个星球上茁壮成长，它帮助创造了这个星球上富含氧气和氮气的大气和适度的温度
